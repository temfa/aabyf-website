About Us

AABYF is a Christian organization that seeks to instill Christian principles and teachings in young people. Our goal is to uplift, inspire, and involve young people in a community that fosters spiritual development and personal progress. Our basic principles of faith, hope, love, and service are what drive our business. Our activities, programs, interactions with the community, and interactions with our young people are all guided by these ideals.

We have established a reputation for excellence in offering educational programs, neighborhood outreach projects, and spiritual direction to young people via our service to our members. Experienced and devoted leaders who are enthusiastic about working with youth make up our team. They are dedicated to assisting the organization's young members in realizing their full potential and contribute a lot of experience and knowledge to the group.

Our Activities:

1

Evangelism

Through the Global Outreach (GO) Movement and Last Quarter Evangelism (LQE), we are dedicated to missions among children and teens. Through these Spirit-filled conversations, thousands of lives have been won for Christ. GO is carried out every year in the month of May across Africa. Last Quarter Evangelism starts from the 1st October till the 31st of December annually.

2

Word & Prayer

Deep knowledge of God's Word and a dynamic prayer life are critical to our fellowship's commitments. As a result, we will continue to campaign for these and will continue to look for innovative methods to improve these vital aspects of our lives as Baptist youth. The AABYF World Day of Prayer is slated for 30th September each year. This serves as a prelude to LQE.

3

Administrative Training (Training):

We continue to show new leaders the route to good leadership through our novel initiative, Administrative Training, by offering skills that will help increase their performance. 4

Cross-language Education (Training):

The AABYF is also concerned with the spread of ideas across the continent. To accomplish this, language barriers must be overcome, and to that end, we are embarking on cross-language training, particularly formal ones such as English, French, and Portuguese, with the hope that this move will enable the Baptist Family to communicate mission, impact, and transformation in an understandable manner across Africa's four walls.

5

BWA Affiliation:

We continue to advocate for Baptist youth to be exposed to a profound grasp of youth chemistry through the BWA Horizons Programme, and we have already raised 29 mentors and several active students. The BWA Horizon Director for Africa is in charge of organizing this.

6

Connections & Engagement

The current Executive Members continue to link Africa's Youth for Christ through in-person and virtual events, particularly at the regional level. Young people are effectively networking as a result of this. We have successfully arranged sub-regional conferences and continue to be present at conventions and unions. After a long day of work and stress, youth need to unwind. Plans are being developed to keep our faith and health fit by exposing us to Christian movies, music, and even aerobic exercises.
